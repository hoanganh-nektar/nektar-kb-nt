Sync latency

Typical end-to-end latency for syncing contacts, activities, and opportunity-contact roles — and what causes delays.

Nektar's design strikes a balance between efficiency and accuracy, tuned to meet real-world CRM data latency requirements without being wasteful of API quotas or compute resources.

Typical end-to-end latency

Operation Average Worst-case
Creating or updating contacts on Salesforce 1 hour 9 hours
Creating or updating activities with participants already on Salesforce 1 hour 9 hours
Creating activities with contacts that need to be created by Nektar 2 hours 18 hours
Creating Opportunity-Contact relations with contacts already on Salesforce 1 hour 9 hours
Creating Opportunity-Contact relations with contacts that need to be created by Nektar 2 hours 18 hours
Merging Nektar-created contacts with manually created duplicates 25 hours 33 hours

The clock starts when an email is sent or received, an event is added or edited, or a manual Salesforce change is made (for self-healing). It stops when Nektar makes the corresponding change on Salesforce.

Something appears to have taken longer

This could happen because:

  • Nektar sync was disabled or stopped due to an error at the time of the event.
  • Nektar sync was only recently enabled for this user or object; these latencies apply to ongoing operations, not during bulk sync. The sync status icon is blue during bulk sync and green during ongoing operations.
  • Events more than 3 months in the future are only processed when they come within the 3-month window.
  • Auto-recall: if there was no matching account at the time of an email but an account was created later, the account creation is the start-clock event — not the email itself.
  • An event was originally filtered out by a screening rule but a subsequent edit made it pass. The edit is the appropriate start-clock event.
  • Salesforce API errors may prevent Nektar writing data for prolonged periods, often caused by poorly performing Apex triggers or other tools consuming too many API calls.
  • In rare circumstances, Nektar, the communication tool's API service, or Salesforce may have encountered a technical incident. Nektar's uptime status for the past 30 days is available at the Nektar status page.

Sources of latency

There are three main sources of latency:

  1. Nektar fetches emails and calendar events periodically from each inbox and calendar, striking a balance at 10–15 minutes to avoid exhausting Google / Microsoft API quotas. (Average delay: 7 minutes; Maximum delay: 15 minutes)
  2. Nektar processes the data: constructing, de-duplicating and linking new activities, contacts and contact roles, as well as re-evaluating all related past data. (Average delay: 15 minutes; Maximum delay: 4 hours in congested conditions)
  3. An intentional 30-minute wait before creates and updates ensures all related records have been independently fetched and unified. Contact merges wait 24 hours. (Wait time: 30 minutes for creates/updates; 24 hours for contact merges)
  4. Nektar writes new data into Salesforce every 10–15 minutes. A fraction of writes fail due to Salesforce congestion and are retried for up to 4 hours. (Average delay: 8 minutes; Maximum delay: 4 hours 15 minutes)
